A tale of fjords, cities and mountains

When people ask me about my favourite memory, many things come to my mind. I recall staring at the fjord, or rather into the fjord, listening to the subtle drumming of raindrops on the surface of the water; I also recall lying on thick snow, looking at an early sunset. My mind also points me towards memories of a train voyage across Norway, surrounded by monumental shards of rock among a vast sea of white.

Many things come to my mind, but most of them share a pattern: they are memories made in nature, far from the busy humming of our bustling society.

I was indeed born in a little village, in northern Spain, a few kilometres away from the French border, in a valley surrounded by some of the highest peaks in southern Europe.
